How to have healthy self-esteem

To boost your self-esteem, you need to identify the negative beliefs
you have about yourself, then challenge them.

You may tell yourself you're "too stupid" to apply for a new job or
that "nobody cares" about you.

Start to note these negative thoughts and write them on a piece
of paper or in a diary. Ask yourself when you first started to think
these thoughts.

Next, start to write some evidence that challenges these negative
beliefs, such as, "I'm really good at cryptic crosswords" or "My sister
calls for a chat every week".

Write down other positive things about yourself, such as "I'm
thoughtful" or "I'm a great cook" or "I'm someone that others trust".

Aim to have at least 5 positive things on your list and add to it
regularly. Then put your list somewhere you can see it. That way,
you can keep reminding yourself that You Are More Than Worthy.

Other ways to improve low self-esteem

  • Recognise what you're good at
  • Build positive relationships
  • Be kind to yourself
  • Learn to be assertive
  • Start saying "no"
  • Give yourself a challenge
